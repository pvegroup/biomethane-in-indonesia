Key Takeaways: Indonesia's Biomethane Development

  • Target Capacity: Indonesia aims to achieve 810 MW of bioenergy by 2025, with biomethane playing a significant role in this transition to cleaner energy.
  • Regional Leaders: Key projects include Asia's largest compressed biogas plant in Langkat Regency (North Sumatra) and the upcoming South Sumatra facility utilizing palm oil waste (POME).
  • Feedstock Diversity: Beyond palm oil waste, Indonesia is expanding into rice husks for biomethane production, reducing CO₂ emissions by over 80% in facilities like the East Java brewery project.
  • Growth Drivers: The biomethane sector is propelled by Indonesia's status as the world's largest palm oil producer, strong government policy support, and increasing private sector interest.
  • Dual Benefits: Biomethane development delivers both economic advantages (reduced imports, job creation) and environmental gains (emissions reduction, improved air quality).
